# About

## Who

My name is Jared, I'm a [redacted] year old software engineer. Most of my career has been spent in service of various distributed compute platforms. I have a wife, four lovely daughters, and two irrepressible grandchildren.

## What

I honestly don't know what I'll be yammering about. My day job is software engineering (my dad, and every other professional engineer, cringes when I say that), but I find myself inexplicably entertained and fascinated by a wide variety of topics. My opinions are just that — mine, and do not *necessarily* represent the views of the company I work for.

## When

Short answer — when the urge to write overcomes my natural inclination towards sloth. Slightly longer answer — I'm going to try and commit to at least once a week. I've tried journaling before, and never ended up making it stick for more than a few days/weeks. Reckon we'll just have to wait and see how it goes this time...

## Where

I currently reside in the great city of Chicago, but I was born, raised, and spent almost all of my life in Texas, where most of my family still resides.

## Why

I have always had the urge to write, and I often have opinions, so those two facts sort of dictate that sooner or later, I'd have a blog. The fact I got to create this with my new best friend, Claudius (my co-worker Tim's name for Claude Code, which I shamelessly stole), is just icing on the cake. Why a beach bar theme? Well, Jimmy Buffett is basically my hero, so that seemed like reason enough.

## How

As I alluded to earlier, I have been heavily using Claude Code this year, with most of my better work being accomplished via [GSD](https://github.com/gsd-build/get-shit-done). That powerful assistance is why this blog exists and isn't still languishing on my to-do list.

## The Wild Meridian

Every good bar needs a name, and every good blog needs one too. Mine comes from Jimmy Buffett's classic tune, The Pascagoula Run. Pull up a stool, order something with salt on the rim, and stay a while.
